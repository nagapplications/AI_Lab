In a bright blue river lived a little fish named Zip. Zip was a fine swimmer — fast as a flicker, graceful as a ribbon. But Zip had one dream.
He wanted to climb a tree.
Every day he watched the squirrels leaping from branch to branch. "That's the life," he sighed, blowing bubbles.
He tried jumping out of the water. Splash. Back in.
He tried grabbing a low branch with his fin. Slip. Back in.
The other fish giggled. "Fish don't climb trees, Zip!"
One afternoon, a tired squirrel named Nell fell into the river with a loud SPLOOSH.
She couldn't swim at all. She thrashed and panicked, going under.
Without thinking, Zip darted beneath her, nudging her toward the bank — guiding her, steadying her, until she grabbed a root and pulled herself out.
Nell lay gasping on the bank. Then she looked at the river. "How did you do that? You moved like magic in there."
Zip blinked. "That's just... swimming. Anyone can do that."
"I can't," said Nell quietly.
For the first time, Zip looked at himself — really looked. Fast. Graceful. Perfectly made for the water.
Maybe, he thought, that was its own kind of wonderful.
Moral: Don't spend so long wishing for someone else's gift that you forget your own.
